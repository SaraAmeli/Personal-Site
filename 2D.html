2D Materials for In-Memory & Neuromorphic Computing

Two-dimensional (2D) materials provide a compelling platform for next-generation computing by enabling co-localized memory and computation. Their atomic thickness, tunable electronic structure, and strong electrostatic control make them particularly suitable for analog computing and neuromorphic architectures.

Device Physics and Mechanisms

Atomristors: Nonvolatile resistance switching in atomically thin materials — Nature Nanotechnology (2019)
Introduces atomristors based on 2D materials, where resistive switching occurs via ion migration and defect dynamics. These devices operate at ultra-low energy scales and are promising for dense in-memory computing arrays.
Ultralow-power artificial synapses using 2D materials — Nature (2020)
Demonstrates synaptic devices based on layered materials exhibiting analog conductance modulation and spike-timing-dependent plasticity (STDP), a key learning rule in biological systems.

Key mechanisms include:

  • Ionic drift and filament formation (memristive switching)
  • Charge trapping/detrapping in van der Waals heterostructures
  • Phase transitions in layered materials
  • Electrostatic gating enabling multi-level conductance states

Crossbar Arrays and In-Memory Computing

Resistive switching memory devices for neuromorphic computing — Nature (2015)
Establishes the crossbar architecture for performing analog matrix-vector multiplication directly in hardware — a cornerstone of in-memory AI acceleration.
A fully integrated analog AI chip — Nature (2019)
Shows large-scale integration of memory-compute units achieving efficient neural network inference, highlighting the practical potential of in-memory computing systems.

2D materials enhance these architectures by:

  • Reducing device footprint → higher density crossbars
  • Enabling analog weight updates
  • Lowering switching energy
  • Supporting heterogeneous integration with CMOS

Neuromorphic Functionality

Neuromorphic computing with memristive devices — Nature Nanotechnology (2020)
Provides a comprehensive overview of memristive synapses and their ability to emulate learning rules such as STDP, short-term plasticity, and long-term potentiation.
Brain-inspired computing with 2D materials — Science (2020)
Explores how atomically thin materials enable biologically realistic neural dynamics, including stochasticity and adaptive learning.

Emerging neuromorphic behaviors include:

  • Spike-based communication
  • Analog synaptic weights
  • Temporal learning dynamics
  • Energy-efficient event-driven processing

Challenges and Open Problems

  • Variability: Device-to-device variation due to atomic defects
  • Endurance: Stability under repeated switching cycles
  • Scalability: Fabrication of uniform large-area 2D layers
  • Integration: Compatibility with existing semiconductor processes

Summary

2D materials enable a fundamentally new computing paradigm by merging memory and computation at the device level. Their unique physical properties allow:
  • Ultra-low-power operation
  • Analog and probabilistic computation
  • Hardware-based learning mechanisms
These characteristics position 2D-material-based devices as key building blocks for future neuromorphic and AI hardware systems.
