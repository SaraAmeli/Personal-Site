Control Theory and Iterative Control

Exploring feedback and feedforward strategies for mastering dynamic systems

Control theory lies at the heart of modern engineering systems—from robotics and electronics to biological and economic models. My work focuses on designing both feedback and feedforward control strategies that enable systems to behave reliably in the presence of uncertainty and nonlinearity.

A particular emphasis of this research is on iterative and digital control mechanisms, especially in systems where discrete-time dynamics and event-based behaviors dominate. These approaches open up new ways to understand synchronization, stability, and emergent patterns in oscillatory systems.

Research Highlights

Below are selected publications that capture key contributions in this area:

  • Arnold’s Tongue for Digital Oscillators

    This work investigates synchronization regions in digital oscillators, revealing how structured parameter spaces influence stability and resonance.

  • Event-Based Model for Digital Oscillators

    A novel modeling framework that captures oscillator dynamics using discrete events, offering improved analytical and computational insights.
